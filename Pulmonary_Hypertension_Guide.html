HxHemodynamic definitions (updated 2022)

Diagnosis requires right heart catheterization. The mPAP threshold was lowered from ≥25 to >20 mmHg, and the PVR threshold for pre-capillary disease from >3 to >2 Wood units.

PAH (Group 1) = pre-capillary hemodynamics (mPAP >20, PAWP ≤15, PVR >2 WU) after excluding groups 2–5. The pulmonary vascular disease—not just the pressure—is what defines it.

DxDiagnostic pathway

Suspect PH from dyspnea + RV signs → echo to assign probability → look for the common causes (groups 2 & 3) → always exclude CTEPH with a V/Q scan → refer to a PH center for right heart catheterization before labeling PAH.

Intermediate/high probability — or low probability with risk factors/unexplained symptoms — warrants further workup.

At diagnosis: 3-strata model (low / intermediate / high) using all data. At follow-up: simplified 4-strata model driven by three variables — the treatment target is to reach and stay low risk.

RxGroup 1 PAH — treatment strategy

Sequence

  • Vasoreactivity test (inhaled NO) only in IPAH/heritable/drug-induced. Responder = mPAP falls ≥10 to <40 mmHg with preserved/↑ cardiac output → trial high-dose CCB (only ~small % remain long-term responders).
  • Initial combination for most (no significant cardiopulmonary comorbidities): ERA + PDE5 inhibitor (e.g. ambrisentan + tadalafil).
  • High risk at diagnosis: add a parenteral prostacyclin (IV epoprostenol/treprostinil) up front — triple therapy; evaluate for transplant.
  • Patients with cardiopulmonary comorbidities: consider initial monotherapy, then individualize.

Reassess & escalate (3–6 months)

  • Re-risk with the 4-strata model. Goal = low risk.
  • Not at low risk → add a drug from an untapped pathway (prostacyclin pathway agent) and/or sotatercept.
  • Sotatercept (Winrevair) activin-signaling inhibitor, SC q3 weeks — approved 2024 as add-on for WHO-FC II–III; 2025 label expansion (ZENITH) to high-risk PAH (76% ↓ in death/transplant/hospitalization).
  • Persistent intermediate-high/high risk → refer for lung transplantation.
General measures: supervised exercise/rehab, diuretics for RV volume overload, O₂ if hypoxemic, iron repletion, pregnancy avoidance/counseling, vaccinations, and referral to an expert PH center. CCBs are only for confirmed vasoreactive responders — harmful otherwise.

2–5Groups 2–5 — key management

Group 2 — left heart disease

  • Treat the underlying HF/valve disease and optimize filling pressures. PAH-specific drugs are not indicated (can cause pulmonary edema). Distinguish IpcPH vs CpcPH by PVR.

Group 3 — lung disease / hypoxia

  • Optimize the lung disease; supplemental O₂ for hypoxemia; treat OSA/OHS.
  • Inhaled treprostinil is approved for PH-ILD (INCREASE trial — improved 6MWD); consider in severe PH (high PVR) at expert centers. Routine oral PAH drugs generally not beneficial and may worsen V/Q matching.

Group 4 — CTEPH

  • Lifelong anticoagulation for all.
  • Pulmonary endarterectomy (PEA) is the treatment of choice for operable disease — potentially curative; decide at a CTEPH center.
  • Inoperable or persistent/recurrent PH after PEA → balloon pulmonary angioplasty (BPA) and/or riociguat (± treprostinil). Multimodal PEA + BPA + medical therapy.

Group 5 — unclear/multifactorial

  • Individualized; treat the underlying condition. Avoid empiric PAH therapy without expert input.

★Attending pearls & pitfalls

Don't miss / don't mislabel

  • Never skip the V/Q scan — CTEPH is potentially curable and CT-PA can miss distal disease.
  • Most PH is Group 2 or 3; don't reflexively start PAH drugs — confirm pre-capillary physiology by RHC first.
  • DLCO out of proportion to lung volumes / isolated low DLCO → suspect pulmonary vascular disease (or CPFE).
  • Suspect PVOD/PCH (a Group 1 subset) if pulmonary edema develops on PAH vasodilators, low DLCO, septal lines + centrilobular ground-glass on CT — refer for transplant.

Practice points

  • Screen high-risk groups: systemic sclerosis (annual DETECT/echo), portal hypertension pre-transplant, BMPR2 carriers.
  • SSc-PAH does worse than IPAH — lower threshold to escalate.
  • Right heart failure signs (rising NT-proBNP, ascites, syncope) = high risk → urgent expert referral.
  • Manage as a PH center problem: combination therapy, parenteral prostacyclins, transplant listing.
