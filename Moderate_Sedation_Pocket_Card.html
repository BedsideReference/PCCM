Moderate (Conscious) Sedation

Adult procedural sedation + critical-care lens · pocket reference
Pre-procedure

Informed Consent sedation-specific

Benefits: comfort · anxiolysis · amnesia Alternatives: local/regional · general anesthesia · defer / none
Risks to disclose: respiratory depression/hypoxia, need for airway support, aspiration, hypotension, drug/allergic reaction, paradoxical reaction, deeper-than-intended sedation, sedation failure; rare cardiorespiratory arrest/death.
Obtain from a decision-capable patient before any sedative is given; confirm NPO status; use surrogate/emergency pathway if capacity impaired.

Indications

  • Endoscopy
  • Cardioversion; TEE
  • Fracture / joint reduction
  • Abscess I&D, laceration/burn care
  • Central line, chest tube, LP, bronchoscopy
  • Imaging requiring stillness + comfort

Before First Dose — SOAP-ME

  • Suction working (Yankauer)
  • Oxygen + capnography
  • Airway: BVM, OPA/NPA, LMA, laryngoscope/ETT
  • Pharmacy: sedatives, reversal, code drugs
  • Monitors: SpO₂, EtCO₂, ECG, BP q5min
  • Equipment: IV, defib, dedicated monitor

NPO / Fasting (elective)

2 h — clear liquids
4 h — breast milk
6 h — formula / light meal / nonhuman milk
8 h — fatty/fried food, meat
Don't cancel solely for clears <2 h (2023 ASA). Assess airway: ASA class · Mallampati · mouth opening · thyromental · neck ROM · dentition · STOP-BANG.
GLP-1 agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide, etc.) delay gastric emptying → aspiration risk despite fasting. Hold weekly agents the week prior, daily agents the day of; if not held or GI symptoms (nausea, bloating, fullness), treat as full stomach — gastric POCUS, RSI precautions, or defer. newer guidance
During

Spectrum of Sedation ASA continuum

MinimalNormal response to voice · airway/vent/CV normal
Moderate ★Purposeful to voice ±touch · airway patent · vent adequate · CV ok
DeepPurposeful only to repeated/painful stim · may need airway support
GeneralUnarousable to pain · airway/vent support usually needed
Depth is unpredictable → must be able to rescue one level deeper than intended. Reflex withdrawal ≠ purposeful.

Medications — Adult IV

Titrate in small increments; wait for peak before re-dosing. ↓ dose 25–50% in elderly, hepatic/renal impairment, and when combining sedative + opioid (synergistic respiratory depression).

Monitoring

  • Continuous SpO₂ + EtCO₂ (capnography catches hypoventilation before desat), ECG, BP q5min, RR, LOC
  • Supplemental O₂; document vitals at set intervals
Rescue

Complications → Fix

  • Hypoventilation/apnea → stimulate, reposition, jaw-thrust, ↑O₂, BVM; pause drug
  • Obstruction → reposition, OPA/NPA, BVM
  • Hypoxia <90% → O₂, assist vent, hold
  • Aspiration → suction, position, support
  • Hypotension → fluids, ↓sedative, pressor
  • Bradycardia → atropine if symptomatic
  • Laryngospasm (ketamine) → jaw-thrust, PPV
  • Chest-wall rigidity ("wooden chest," rapid/high-dose fentanyl) → stop opioid; naloxone (reverses it); BVM w/ 100% O₂; if refractory → NMB (succinylcholine/rocuronium) + intubate. Benzo = adjunct sedation only (won't relax the rigidity). Prevent: give opioid slowly.

Reversal — Rescue, Not Routine

  • First-line for over-sedation = airway + O₂ + ventilation (stimulate, jaw-thrust, BVM). Reversal supplements support.
  • Naloxone 0.04–0.4 mg IV q2–3min, titrate to respiration. Dur 30–60 min → watch re-sedation (monitor ≥2 h).
  • Flumazenil 0.2 mg IV over 15s, repeat q1min (max ~1 mg). Seizure risk in benzo-dependent / TCA co-ingestion.
Recovery

Recovery

  • Monitor until baseline mental status & vitals; watch re-sedation — reversal agents are shorter-acting than the drugs they reverse
  • Confirm protective airway reflexes returned before reducing monitoring
Critical care

Critical-Care Lens ICU

Procedural sedation in the critically ill
  • Low reserve → exaggerated hypotension & desaturation; pre-oxygenate, have vasopressors + airway ready
  • Unstable? Favor ketamine (preserves BP/drive) or etomidate (hemodynamically neutral) over propofol/benzo boluses
  • Common ICU procedures: DC cardioversion, bronchoscopy, chest tube, central/arterial line, trach, wound care, EGD/TEE
  • Already intubated → sedation goal is comfort + tolerance, not airway protection; still monitor hemodynamics/EtCO₂
RASS — quick scale
+4 combative · +3 very agitated · +2 agitated · +1 restless · 0 alert/calm · −1 drowsy · −2 light sedation · −3 moderate · −4 deep · −5 unarousable
Most vented patients: aim 0 to −2.
Consider high-risk if: ASA III–IV, frailty, or age extremes · difficult airway (Mallampati III–IV, short thyromental, limited neck ROM, OSA) · hemodynamic instability or severe cardiopulmonary disease · full stomach / inadequate NPO / high aspiration risk · prior adverse sedation reaction.
